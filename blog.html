GenScript Opens New Logistics Hub in Sydney

GenScript opens a new operations site and logistics center in Sydney to improve service for Australia’s biotechnology sector....

The Sydney facility is designed to enhance GenScript’s logistical capabilities, offering Australian researchers faster and more efficient access to essential research reagents. By providing a local supply chain, GenScript aims to eliminate obstacles in the procurement process, such as customs clearance and import permits, which can delay research progress. One standout feature of the new site is its next-day shipping service for high-demand products, including mRNA, growth factors, and Cas proteins. “Establishing a logistics hub in Sydney enables us to deliver more localised support to our Australian partners,” stated Sherry Shao, GenScript’s rotating CEO. “This expansion is about building long-term relationships within the region’s biotech ecosystem, allowing our customers to focus entirely on advancing their research.”

Pharmaceutical Logistics Market

An overview of the pharmaceutical logistics category for pharmaceutical procurement teams. This guide provides you...

The global pharmaceutical logistics market is a rapidly growing sector that is expected to reach a market size of USD $170.17 billion by 2025, with a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 9.2% from 2023 to 2030. The Asia Pacific region is expected to experience the highest growth rate during this period due to the rising demand for pharmaceutical products and increasing investment in healthcare infrastructure.

The pharmaceutical logistics market can be further broken down by transportation mode, including air freight, sea freight, and road freight. Air freight is the most commonly used mode of transportation for pharmaceutical products due to its speed and reliability, and it is expected to continue to dominate the market in the coming years.
